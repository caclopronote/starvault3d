Introduction

Welcome to the first galactic cartography site...

No no, you read well, these pages are going to offer you an unpublished point of view on the space that surrounds us, at the level galactic, thanks to a representation in 3D of stars situated in a radius of about 200 light-year (in a first time), in order to integrate these in the setting of a new cartography for which we invite you to participate.

However, for once in the history of the cartography, it is in fact improper to speak of radius, since this is not a heliocentric representation, that is to say that even though stars have been represented from the usual coordinates, right ascension and declension defined from the carving of the sky of the Earth, the Sun has a central room by no means in all this.

I think that in spite of all these explanations you are again in the fuzzy to the goal of this site, and we go therefore of the to presses to expose you thanks to some illustrations this news galactic cartography.

 

·         first of all here it is, to the biggest scale, the carving in four parts of the Milky Way. I don't hide myself that this one was me inspired by the famous set of science-fiction that known by everybody, I think, that cuts up the Galaxy also in four pieces having each a Greek letter to name it. Even though we don't have the right to take the name that they use for these pieces again, I think that we can keep the four Greek letters a, b, g and d to name them.  

·         On this first illustration you can see the four quarters of galaxy a , b, g and d that don't have any definite rooms again on artist's view. The only decision that has already been taken, and I think that you are going all to be okay with us, is that the part where our Sun takes place being named a .

·         The small white cube is the smallest whole that you can meet in the galaxy. It represents 1/1000th of the cube in which it is. We will call this whole of 20 light-year cube a Sector and it is here that you intervene. . . Indeed, all cubes, some either the scale, have some definite coordinates from the median plan of the galaxy in the horizontal plan (in red on the diagram), that is to say that one cuts the galaxy in two to define some positive vertical coordinates (if one is above of the median plan) or negative (if one is underneath). They also have some definite coordinates from the sagittals plans (in blue on the diagram) and vertical (in green on the diagram) of the milky way that are, as for has them, inevitably positive.
